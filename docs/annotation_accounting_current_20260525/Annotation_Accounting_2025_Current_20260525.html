Local projections estimate dynamic responses through a separate regression at each horizon after a policy intervention or shock. The outcome dated t + h is regressed on the shock dated t, conditional on controls, and the resulting sequence of horizon-specific coefficients gives the impulse response. This approach follows Jorda (2005) and the later local projection literature, which estimates impulse responses directly without imposing the full dynamic restrictions of a vector autoregression (Jorda and Taylor, 2024). The public investment shock is identified before the local projection regressions are estimated, because a change in public investment is not automatically a policy shock.
